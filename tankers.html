üíß Understanding pH Levels in Water

What is pH? pH measures how acidic or alkaline water is on a scale of 0-14. A pH of 7 is neutral, below 7 is acidic, and above 7 is alkaline.

Why it matters: For drinking water, the ideal pH range is 6.5-8.5 (BIS & WHO standards). This ensures the water is safe, tastes good, and doesn't corrode pipes. For construction water, pH 6-8 is acceptable as it prevents concrete damage and corrosion.
